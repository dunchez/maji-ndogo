# Project README: Maji Ndogo Water Restoration Initiative

## Project Overview
The Maji Ndogo project is a comprehensive, data-driven mission to resolve a national water crisis. By analyzing a massive database of 60,000 records—collected by engineers, scientists, and field workers—this project identifies critical gaps in water accessibility and quality. The ultimate goal is to move from raw data exploration to actionable engineering solutions that provide clean, reliable water to every citizen.

## Problem Statement
Maji Ndogo faces a multi-faceted water challenge. Many citizens rely on contaminated wells or distant rivers, while those with access to shared taps often face wait times that far exceed international standards. Furthermore, infrastructure decay and a lack of centralized tracking have historically hindered repair efforts. This project was launched to uncover the ground truth and create a transparent, efficient roadmap for recovery.

## Technical Framework
The project is built upon a robust MySQL database designed to manage complex relationships between locations, water sources, and community needs. Key components of the data architecture include:
* **Water Source Management**: Categorization of all functional and non-functional sources, including home taps, shared taps, wells, and rivers.
* **Pollution Tracking**: Detailed logs of biological and chemical contaminants used to prioritize high-risk areas.
* **Logistics & Usage**: Analysis of visitation patterns and queue times to identify where the current infrastructure is failing to meet demand.
* **Geographic Mapping**: Linking water sources to specific provinces and towns to ensure equitable resource distribution.

## Project Phases

### 1. Data Exploration & Standardization
The initial phase involves deep-diving into the database to understand the foundational tables. Analysts standardize the data, identify unique source types, and begin the process of "listening to the story" told by the records. This stage ensures that the data is clean and ready for advanced querying.

### 2. Pattern Analysis & Clustering
By moving beyond individual data points, the project identifies broader narratives. This includes analyzing wait times at shared taps and comparing them against the UN standard of 30 minutes. Clustering the data helps decision-makers see the "bird's eye view" of which provinces are suffering the most from infrastructure neglect.

### 3. Integrity Auditing & Anti-Corruption
To ensure the plan is based on truth, an independent audit is conducted. This phase is critical for identifying inconsistencies between field reports and actual conditions. A major breakthrough in this stage involves using SQL to uncover a data-tampering scandal, where specific officials were found to have manipulated records for personal gain. Neutralizing this corruption is essential for the project's credibility.

### 4. Engineering & Implementation
In the final phase, the analysis is converted into a master "job list." A dedicated progress table is created to track specific engineering tasks, such as:
* Installing UV and reverse osmosis filters for contaminated wells.
* Repairing broken infrastructure and defunct pipes.
* Installing new taps in high-density areas to reduce wait times.
* Coordinating water tankers as short-term relief for the busiest locations.

## Final Deliverables
* **Validated Database**: A clean, audited repository of national water data.
* **Project Progress Tracker**: A functional system for engineers to manage repairs and installations.
* **Executive Summary**: A high-level report for national leadership to assist with budget allocation and long-term infrastructure planning.

## Conclusion
Maji Ndogo: From Analysis to Action is more than a technical exercise; it is a commitment to using data for the public good. By combining rigorous SQL analysis with a transparent auditing process, the project provides a definitive path toward water security and prosperity for all.
